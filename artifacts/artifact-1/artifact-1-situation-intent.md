# Situation
The Fellowship is in the early-to-mid stage of their journey after departing Rivendell, moving through unfamiliar and hostile territory. They face fragmented knowledge about routes, uncertain threats, and limited ability to coordinate effectively across changing conditions.
Their main difficulty is not simply getting from A to B, but making informed decisions under uncertainty:
Routes may be dangerous, blocked, or misleading,
Information is incomplete, outdated, or held by different members
This problem is non-trivial because no single member has complete knowledge of the situation. Decisions require combining partial knowledge, interpreting risks, and adapting quickly. 
# Intent
The Fellowship Companion is a shared situational communication application for Fellowship members.
It enables them to communicate critical journey information—such as location, threats, and plans—in a structured and consistent way, improving group coordination under uncertain conditions.
It does not replace leadership decisions or provide automated guidance.
# Value
The Fellowship gains a reliable way to share and align critical information, reducing misunderstandings and ensuring that all members operate with the same situational awareness.
By structuring communication, the application minimizes the risk of:
Important details being lost, misremembered, or unevenly distributed
Conflicting interpretations of routes, threats, or plans
Delays caused by repeated clarification or misalignment
Without this tool:
Communication would rely on memory and assumptions
Information would spread inconsistently across the group
Decisions would be slower and more error-prone due to miscommunication
Overall, the application increases coordination efficiency, reduces avoidable mistakes, and allows the Fellowship to act more cohesively in high-risk situations.
# System Capabilities 
1. Structured Information Sharing
The system enables Fellowship members to communicate critical information in a clear and consistent format:
Locations, routes, and movement updates
Observed threats or anomalies
Intentions and planned actions

1. Shared Communication Context:
The system maintains a common, evolving record of communicated information:
What has been said, by whom, and when
Updates or corrections to prior information
Visibility into the current agreed understanding
This prevents information loss and avoids reliance on individual memory.
1. Information Clarification and Alignment:
The system supports resolving misunderstandings by:
Highlighting conflicting or unclear information
Allowing members to confirm or revise shared messages
Making differences in interpretation visible
This helps the group reach alignment before acting.
1. Prioritization of Critical Communication:
The system helps users focus on what matters most by:
Distinguishing urgent vs non urgent information
Emphasizing high-risk updates 
Reducing noise from less relevant communication
This ensures important messages are not overlooked.
1. Communication Under Constraints:
The system supports effective communication despite limitations:
Partial or delayed message exchange
Incomplete information
Situations where not all members are present
This enables continuity of understanding even when communication is fragmented.
1. Coordination Through Shared Updates:
The system enables members to stay aligned on group actions:
Communicating intentions and changes in plans,
tracking who is aware of which updates,
supporting coordinated movement and decision execution.
# Assumptions & Constraints 
* Incomplete and Unreliable Information:
Not all terrain, threats, or paths are known; some information may be outdated or incorrect.
* Limited Communication:
The Fellowship cannot always communicate instantly or at a distance; synchronization may be delayed.
* High Cognitive Load on Users:
Users are under stress, fatigue, and time pressure. 
Limited attention for composing or interpreting messages
Risk of miscommunication due to haste or exhaustion
Need for communication to be concise and easily understood
* Minimal Technological Support:
The communication system operates under constrained conditions:
No guarantee of constant connectivity or synchronization
Limited ability to process, verify, or enrich information automatically
Must function with simple, lightweight interaction.
